# Category Playbook

Use this file to choose category-specific cost buckets and evidence priorities.

## Consumer Electronics

Primary buckets:

- BOM / components
- Manufacturing / assembly
- R&D amortization
- Logistics
- Marketing
- Channel markup
- Warranty / after-sales
- Tax
- Brand premium
- Net profit

Best evidence:

- Teardown/BOM posts
- Chip/component quotes
- Company gross margin
- Supplier annual reports
- Retail channel take rates

Common trap: confusing BOM with total cost. Electronics often have meaningful warranty, R&D, channel, and marketing costs.

## Beauty / Skincare

Primary buckets:

- Formula/raw materials
- Packaging
- Manufacturing
- R&D/formulation
- Marketing/KOL/celebrity
- Channel markup
- Brand premium
- Tax
- Net profit

Best evidence:

- Parent company financials
- OEM/ODM quotes
- Ingredient market prices
- Channel commission rates

Common trap: saying "raw material is cheap, so product is scam" without discussing packaging, marketing, distribution, and brand economics.

## Luxury / Fashion

Primary buckets:

- Materials
- Craft/manufacturing
- Design
- Retail/store network
- Marketing/PR
- Brand premium
- Tax/import duties
- Net profit

Best evidence:

- Group annual reports
- Gross margin disclosures
- Leather/textile supply estimates
- Retail footprint costs

Common trap: treating luxury as functional goods. The premium is the product, but the report should still show its size.

## Food / Restaurant

Primary buckets:

- Ingredients
- Labor
- Rent
- Utilities
- Platform commission
- Packaging/delivery
- Marketing
- Tax
- Net profit

Best evidence:

- Menu price versus ingredient market price
- Foodservice gross margin ranges
- Platform commission rates
- Store rent and labor estimates

Common trap: using home-cooking ingredient cost as the full benchmark.

## SaaS / Software

Primary buckets:

- Infrastructure / cloud
- Engineering / R&D
- Sales and marketing
- Customer success/support
- Compliance/security
- Payment fees
- Admin
- Net margin

Best evidence:

- Company financials
- S-1 / IPO prospectus
- Cloud pricing
- Public SaaS benchmark reports

Common trap: saying "software has no marginal cost" while ignoring R&D, CAC, support, and compliance.

## Education / Training

Primary buckets:

- Instructor/coach labor
- Curriculum development
- Sales/marketing/CAC
- Platform/classroom
- Operations
- Refund/service reserve
- Tax
- Profit

Best evidence:

- Public company financials
- Course platform fee schedules
- Teacher salary/rate data
- CAC benchmarks

Common trap: only counting teacher hours and ignoring enrollment conversion costs.

## Medical Beauty / Healthcare Service

Primary buckets:

- Consumables/drugs
- Doctor/operator labor
- Equipment amortization
- Rent/decoration
- Licensing/compliance
- Marketing/CAC
- Follow-up/service
- Tax
- Profit

Best evidence:

- Device price and lifespan
- Clinic chain financials
- Consumable quotes
- Local service price comparisons

Common trap: giving medical advice. Keep the analysis to price/cost structure and advise professional consultation for medical decisions.

## Furniture / Home

Primary buckets:

- Raw materials
- Manufacturing
- Design/R&D
- Warehousing
- Logistics/installation
- Retail channel
- Marketing
- Tax
- Profit

Best evidence:

- Material price ranges
- Factory quotes
- Listed company margins
- Platform/retail commission

Common trap: ignoring bulky logistics and return/replacement costs.

## EV / Vehicles

Primary buckets:

- Battery pack
- Motors/electronics
- Body/chassis
- Manufacturing
- R&D
- Sales/service network
- Warranty
- Tax/subsidy
- Net profit or loss

Best evidence:

- Automaker financials
- Battery pack cost reports
- Supplier pricing
- Public teardown estimates

Common trap: using component cost alone to infer automaker profit. Many EVs sell with thin profit or losses after R&D and channel costs.
